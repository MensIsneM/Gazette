General Questions

What is a Gazette notification?

A Gazette notification is an official government publication used to publicly record legal changes such as name change and gender change.

ⓘ More Info

Once published, the notification becomes part of public government records and can later be used for updating documents such as Aadhaar, PAN, Passport and bank records.

Last reviewed: May 2026

Is this website official government website?

No. This is an independent informational project created to simplify the Gazette process.

Official Gazette publications are handled through government portals such as egazette.gov.in and BharatKosh.

Last reviewed: May 2026

Why was this website created?

The Gazette process is confusing, fragmented and difficult for many people to understand. This project aims to simplify the process using guides, generators and tools.

Last reviewed: May 2026

Is this guide free?

Yes. The guide and generator are provided free of cost.

Some government fees, newspaper charges, printing costs and travel expenses still apply separately.

Eligibility & Legal Questions

Do I need surgery for Gazette notification?

No. Surgery is generally not required for Gazette publication.

ⓘ More Info

Current Gazette workflows are usually based on declarations and supporting documentation rather than proof of surgery itself.

Last reviewed: May 2026

Can I change both name and gender together?

Yes. Both name and gender changes are commonly combined into a single Gazette notification.

Last reviewed: May 2026

Do I need court order?

Usually no. Most Gazette applications are processed without requiring a court order.

Last reviewed: May 2026

Can minors apply?

Minor applications may require parent or guardian involvement and additional documentation.

Minor procedures may differ significantly. Please verify current legal requirements carefully.

Last reviewed: May 2026

Can I apply from another state?

Yes. Central Gazette applications are commonly submitted by people living across different states.

ⓘ More Info

Address proof and supporting documents are generally more important than the state you currently reside in.

Last reviewed: May 2026

What is the difference between Central Gazette and State Gazette?

Central Gazette is handled through the Department of Publication under the Government of India, while State Gazettes are handled separately by individual states.

State Gazette procedures vary heavily between states and may not always support gender and name change together.

Last reviewed: May 2026

Documents & Requirements

What documents are usually required?
  • Identity proof
  • Address proof
  • Affidavit
  • Newspaper advertisement
  • Passport photos
  • GID/GD letter or supporting documentation
  • Government fee receipt

Last reviewed: May 2026

What is a GID/GD letter?

It generally refers to supporting medical or psychological documentation related to gender dysphoria/gender identity.

Requirements may change over time depending on administrative practice and legal updates.

Last reviewed: May 2026

Is newspaper advertisement mandatory?

In most cases, yes. Newspaper publication is commonly included as part of the process.

Last reviewed: May 2026

Why is CD required?

The Department of Publication traditionally requests soft-copy document submission through CD media.

Requirements may modernize in future, but CD submission is still commonly used currently.

Last reviewed: May 2026

Can I use black and white photocopies?

Usually yes for supporting photocopies, unless specifically requested otherwise.

Last reviewed: May 2026

Timeline & Publication

How long does Gazette publication take?

Publication may take several weeks depending on workload, holidays, submission method and verification.

In-person applications are often processed faster than postal applications.

Last reviewed: May 2026

How do I know if my Gazette is published?

You must regularly check weekly Gazette PDF releases through the eGazette portal.

ⓘ More Info

Search terms such as "gender as" inside Part IV weekly Gazette PDFs may help locate entries faster.

Last reviewed: May 2026

Why can’t I find my name?

Publication may still be pending, delayed or placed inside a different weekly issue.

Do not panic immediately. Delays of multiple weeks are common.

Last reviewed: May 2026

Which Gazette category should I search?

Most gender and name change notifications are usually published under:

  • Weekly Gazette
  • Part IV

Last reviewed: May 2026

Privacy & Safety

Is Gazette public?

Yes. Gazette notifications are public government records.

Please understand that old and new names may become publicly searchable after publication.

Last reviewed: May 2026

Will my old name remain visible?

Usually yes. Gazette notifications generally contain both previous and updated details.

Last reviewed: May 2026

Can employers or others search it?

Potentially yes, because Gazette publications are public records.

Last reviewed: May 2026

Can Gazette publication be removed later?

Usually no. Gazette publications are intended to remain part of public records once published.
