What is a Gazette?

A Gazette is an official government publication used to notify the public about laws, policy changes, and important developments in India. It is also used for publishing personal changes such as name corrections or name changes. After the enactment of the Transgender Persons (Protection of Rights) Act, 2019, the Central Gazette (2020) introduced a process for legally recognizing a person’s gender identity under the Act. The procedure is broadly similar to a name-change notification, but it requires additional documents and specific conditions.

Prerequisites

    Before starting your application, ensure you meet the following requirements:

  • You are at least 18 years of age
  • Have a GD/GID letter from a registered medical practitioner
  • Have a valid email ID and phone number
  • Have a proof of identity
  • Have proof of address
  • Have sufficient funds to cover application and related costs
